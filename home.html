The Basics

A few things to keep in mind

  • This website does not constitute legal advice, it is simpy an organized database of information to help point you in the right direction.
  • Export controls are designed to not only protect U.S. national security interests, but also allow U.S. companies to engage in commercial activities.
  • All companies must seek authorization before exporting controlled technologies. This could mean a license, agreement, or license exception.
  • Most export controls are based on the technical capabilities of a technology, not on its intended use.
  • Items on the USML (controlled by ITAR) are generally technologies, information, or services that could be used for threatening foreign military purposes.
  • Items on the CCL (controlled by EAR) are applicable to both commercial and military use.
